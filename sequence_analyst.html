Career: Sequence Analyst


This job is the middle ground between a research biologist and a data scientist. You do not need strong coding skills, as an elementary understanding of coding to utilize the developed packages would suffice your daily tasks. However, this career requires a strong biology background; often, you need to be as fluent in biology as the rest of your research peers because key insights can only be spotted if you thoroughly understand the research topic you are analyzing.

This is the easiest transition if you are from the field of biology. You can also take full advantage of your interest in the sub-field of biology that you wish to research in.

Career Specific Advice


In addition to the courses you would take from the sub-field of biology you are interested in, you would also want to take some introductory coding course, such as

  1. More advanced statistics course
  2. Introductory coding, in Python or R
  3. Introductory computer tools usage, bash shell scripting, github, etc.
Another good starting point is to take an introductory course to general bioinformatics. Most schools have a course set up for general biology students without any coding experience, for them to gain some fundamental knowledge in bioinformatics. (eg. in UCSD, it is BIMM 140).
